Robotics researcher and engineer working at the intersection of mechanical design, embedded sensing, and learned control. My work focuses on tendon-driven continuum robots, how they sense, model, and respond to contact with the physical world.

I hold a BASc in Engineering Science (Electrical and Computer Engineering) from the University of Toronto and am currently completing a MASc in Mechanical Engineering at the [Continuum Robotics Laboratory](https://crl.utm.utoronto.ca/) under Professor Jessica Burgner-Kahrs. I approach robotics from a systems engineering perspective from CAD and physical prototyping to control theory, simulation, and reinforcement learning. I'm drawn to problems where the hardware and software have to be co-designed to work.

## Research Interests
- Contact-aware motion planning and control for continuum robots
- Embedded sensing and state estimation for compliant manipulators
- Reinforcement learning for contact-rich manipulation
- Open-source robot platform development
